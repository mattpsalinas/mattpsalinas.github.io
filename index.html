Research Interests

Fault Slip-Rate Variability

Recent paleoseismological research has shown that in the Marlboro Plate Boundary Fault System (New Zealand), complementary faults trade slip with time, where individual faults experience periods of fast and slow slip, but the system maintains a near constant slip rate. Can this phenomenon be observed elsewhere, and if so, what can it tell us about fault system mechanics?

Blind-Thrust Paleoseismology

Blind thrust faults are both dangerous and difficult to study owing to the fact that they do not reach the surface. Several famous earthquakes have occurred on blind thrust faults (1994 Northridge, 1999 Chi-Chi, 2008 Wenchuan), which highlight the need for a more complete understanding of blind thrust faults. I have an ongoing project studying the paleoseismology of the southern segment of the Compton Thrust, an active blind thrust fault beneath metropolitan Los Angeles.

Geothermal Induced Seismicity

Completed Honors Thesis with Prof. Yihe Huang at the University of Michigan, studying the physical mechanisms controlling geothermal induced seismicity through the lens of spatiotemporal b-value analysis.
